Here is the application test for our Calculus Camp. This test is intended to be difficult and we do NOT expect applicants to be able to solve all the problems. Even if you get stuck on a problem, please still show your thought process. We are mainly interested in your ability to think mathematically and express your ideas on paper. As such, the use of calculators or outside resources (i.e. textbooks) is prohibited. Good luck and enjoy the problems!

Problem 1

Simplify the fraction $$\frac{1}{\sqrt{x-4\sqrt{x-4}}}$$ so that there are no square roots in the denominator of your answer.

Problem 2

Simplify the following expression: $$\log(20^{21})+\log\left({10^{2^{0^{2^1}}}}\right).$$ In this problem, $a^{b^c}=a^{(b^c)}$.

Problem 3

Region $R$ contains all of the points $(x,y)$ with $x,y>0$ such that $x+y\le \frac{13\pi}{2}$ and $\cos(x+y)\ge 0$. Find the area of $R$.
