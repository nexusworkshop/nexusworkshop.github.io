Dedication

Have you ever spent hours pouring over a math concept only for it to remain as undecipherable as hieroglyphics? We've all been there, and that's why we take pride in Nexus' clear step-by-step explanations of every math concept and example problem. We won't skip steps, and we keep prerequisites at a minimum. Each of our articles takes dozens of hours to write before being carefully proofread. We value accuracy, so that's why you should definitely let us know if you find an error.

Accesible

Nexus was built with learners in mind. This means that we do our absolute best to make our site as informative, accessible, and easy to navigate as possible. All of our articles are broken down into bite-sized pieces. It's even possible for you to learn on the go - if you don't have enough time to read the whole article, just open a single toggle!

Engaging

We know that spending hours staring at math textbooks comprised of dead tree can be incredibly boring and overwhelming, so our articles aim to fix that. So we've got the solution! We've highlighted and color-coded all the important stuff for you so that you no longer have to worry about picking out details while your brain is being bombarded with information. Our site is both minimalistic and aesthetic, and we hope that the occasional splash of color will help boost learners' motivation.

Welcoming

Yes, we know. Every site claims to be home to a special community. But we also know that online communities can easily become toxic. The Nexus team believes that learning math should feel inspiring, not oppressive. That's why we've created a carefully moderated Discord Server for those who join our mailing list. The term "mathletes" was coined for a reason, and we believe that all members of the math community have a responsibility to support each other. Everyone belongs here, no matter your skill level or contest scores.


Mission Statement

Our goal at Nexus is to:

  • Spark the curiosity of fellow students and legends
  • Connect them with a welcoming community
  • Allow them to make discoveries that go beyond what's offered in the classroom

We believe that math should revolve around creativity, so our articles aim to expose learners to a variety of unusual topics for them to explore and make connections.

Our resources are and always will be at an extremely affordable price because we feel that education should be easily accessible for everyone.
